Kavukamari wrote:
orngjce223 wrote:
Incidentally, how does your species handle reproduction?

Jz: you feel an undying urge to definitely think about this :P

Usually children are designed by taking the parents' genomes, updating them to match the desired variant and version of the genome, combining them in two ways to produce two gametes, and implanting one gamete in each parent. If for some reason the required technology were not available, mutual fertilisation of one another through copulation is possible too, but is a bad idea in normal circumstances since the resulting children will have out-of-date genomes. Fertilisation of oneself is an even worse idea, unless one is the only person left in the world. In any case, each parent lays a hard-shelled egg, which must be incubated until hatching.
