Dfaran wrote:
>Is this something that happens all the time, an unfortunate coincidence, or astronomically unlikely?

About a third of the time there is a stabiliser being maintained. Occasionally, the beginnings of a problem is spotted by the control systems of one and it must be shut down for actual repair. The two events have coincided before, which is why there are 3 spare stabilisers not just 2.

However, the way that one has just stopped without warning and all diagnostics have gone off-line is not common at all. No problem was detected before the failure, especially nothing that would take out the communications and the stabilisation system at the same time. It was only being used very lightly at the time since it was acting as a backup stabiliser.
