So, I thought I'd have a go at running an adventure. The tone is mostly serious; the genre is some kind of sci-fi. The "art" is schematic, the idea being that it is the normal style of drawing for the protagonist's culture. I am dumping readers in at the deep end somewhat; figuring out what this place is like is part of the puzzle I guess. BTW, you can ask the protagonist questions as well as giving commands, though I can't guarantee a useful response.



Your name is Tkhlpzyv. You are a citizen compiled from a rather conservative codebase. Your job is to locate and retrieve feral individuals so that others can study the effects of isolation or unusual upbringing on their minds, to bring new concepts into the long-term planning of the species-engineering team.

You have just ended a period of rest and maintenance, and turned off your bed. You must now prepare to head from your room in Su-0-0, to Su-4-7, where your next subject is.

magicalsushi wrote:
Tk: Ponder the potentially Cartesian nature of the co-ordinates Su-0-0 and Su-4-7, and wonder what happened to the other three dimensions.

You recall the meta-cosmology you studied years ago. The multiverse has an approximately-Euclidean 5D space, which is the cross-product of the 3 dimensions along which each parallel subuniverse extends ("x", "y", and "z") and the 2 dimensions that separate them ("u" and "v").

Warning: this diagram contains sections that require an order of space greater than that supported by your rendering device. Information loss may occur or some features may not be available.

This is a 2D subuniverse arrangement, and its chi-product with a 3D subuniverse shape, producing a 5D space containing parallel subuniverses.

The subuniverses are named after their coordinates (in nanometres) relative to the home universe, so the home subuniverse is Su-0-0, and today you are going to the one called Su-4-7, if you ever stop reminiscing about elementary geometry.
