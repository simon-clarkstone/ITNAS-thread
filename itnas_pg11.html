Crowstone wrote:
And what is the black circle around the computer block do again?
You wonder why different aspects of the pedestal you encountered earlier are reached by two different power and data routes. Also, the barrier that was stopping you accessing much of the network when plugged into the pedestal is not a barrier at all when viewed from the building's power supply.



The main column continues starting up. It makes a some sort of external communications device attached to it available so have an internet connection. You feel like a truely moral being again, now that you are connected to the needs and values of civilisation as a whole. How can one be moral if one does not have others' knowledge of what is needed and expertise of what the detailed consequences of actions will be?

You look up the correct usage of the cable-terminating tool you were using badly earlier, and train a couple of modules of your mind using the lessons.
Crowstone wrote:
Would a data cable have actual data in it?
You look this up too, and find that the values vary, but the sort you are using now has about 10Mb in each metre, consisting of the almost same piece of firmware repeated hundreds of times in the computing dust embedded in it. The cable's internal network is not really of interest to you though. It says that you joined the cable poorly, but you knew that already, and you don't need a precise 3D diagram of just how badly you did it. The information of real interest to you is that being transmitted through the cable, which turns out to be about 1Mb per metre at any instant, if you multiply the transmission rate by the time taken for light to travel that far through the cable.

Thinking of which, what do you need to do now you are connected back to the internet and the rest of civilisation?

The author can take frivolous suggestions and subvert them, as well as sensible suggestions.
