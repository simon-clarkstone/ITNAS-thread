    TK: Document some more flaws in Jays work, and then find a good work-around for the aftermath.
    Originally Posted by SonicLover
    Not much I can say, except that I wouldn't say no to a recap.


    You decide that this would be a good time to reflect on what has happened over the hours since your last proper rest and what you and Jzaprvlt have done. Such introspection is vital to spotting of systematic weaknesses.

    You intended to travel from Su-0-0 (the origin subuniverse) to Su-4-7 to retrieve a feral indivial known as LN-4-7-22, who had been placed there 8 years ago to test new anti-psychomodification-lockout systems incorporated into it, as well as for general study of the effects on development. You rose, picked up your tools, and arranged to meet up with Jzaprvlt over in Su-4-7. As you set out you discussed some translation code with Vcypcsua and found out that Jz had been making some poorly-planned changes to it without telling you. Vcypcsua had been looking at the code to help with Vc's work on subjects that you had previous retrieved, especially LN-4-7-18.

    You started flying in the direction of the spaceport, but on your way something (quite possibly some Aphroditans, though you didn't know of them at the time) hit you with an EMP, destroying your radio capabilities and knocking you unconscious. Upon waking you were too tired to take off, so you headed towards a small building nearby. You eventually learnt it had (at least) two functions:
    • an anchor-point for the ascent-structure at the spaceport you were heading to,
    • and a container for a project kept secret to avoid it being slowed down by over-resourcing: a supercomputer attached to a helmet capable of direct concept injection into the mind.

    You re-fitted the one block of computronium that had been removed from a rack of the supercomputer, then donned the helmet. It paralysed you by design, and after struggling with the interface you resorted to cutting power to most of the building in order to disconnect.
    Using the helmet system was rather dangerous in retrospect. You might have been better off just waiting until someone turned up. Also cutting the power with more precision would have been a good idea, and especially that for the anchor point.
    The power-off also removed a barrier in a doorway, thus allowing you into the next room, where you mis-adjusted the controls for the cooling systems of the anchor-point and were able to get internet access to tell Jzaprvlt what was going on.
    Again not something you should have been fiddling with.
    The powering-off and cooling system misconfiguration indirectly delayed Jz's upward journey from the equivalent spaceport over in Su-x8-5.

    There were some lifts and dumbwaiters leading to/from below the building, but their power cables were damaged and you never found out what was down there. You flew onward to the spaceport, where you had surgery performed by Ywsaznxd to help your recovery (which will take days even with that help).

    (You realise that Ywsaznxd is watching you as you write this; you wonder why.)

    Jzaprvlt had decided to go over to Su-4-7 without you. After locating LN-4-7-22, Jz found that it was friends with a young human, but did not consider the (true) possiblity that this fact had been concealed from the rest of the humans and that they would be afraid of and/or aggressive towards the subject.
    Extensive experience of human nature and society, which you have and Jz does not, is needed to comprehend such complex outcomes. Letting Jz go out alone was a mistake. Jz has only been on a few missions before, always accompanied by you.

    The subject and the young human had noticed the spacecraft descending, and Jz arranged that they would encounter it and enter. (The human called Dahnel Jones must also have noticed the descent.) From monitoring them, Jzaprvlt was able to tell something about how the subject's mind had developed: some parts of it are almost disconnected from the parts that have control of the body. This is a success; such division is the anti-PML safety mechanism being tested, to stop the subject from psychomodifying itself (at around age 4) into a state which it could not escape and which would kill it. Normal configs will suffer terminal PML 56/60 of the time without supervision (and about 7/60 of the time even if supervised). The subject has also imitated some human properties such as sleep, which will be worth studying even if less obviously useful.

    Upon leaving the spacecraft, LN-4-7-22 took a REMI tool with it, and accidentally used it to render the human unconscious. While trying to take the young human back to safety, the subject encountered Jzaprvlt, who induced strong attraction sensations into its mind to help control it. The effects were strong enough that they will cause problems later when anaylsing the subject; Jzaprvlt will have to be present continuously at first.
    That effect is certainly very skillful, but it really wasn't the right solution. Concentrating on the input that Jz was getting from the sensors Jz had deployed could have have stopped what happened next.

    Jzaprvlt and the subject encountered Dahnel, thereby confirming Da's suspicions that you were Aresians (the name is inaccurate, because it literally means that you are from Mars, but that is what your species were considered to be by these humans
    back when you first invaded them 68 years ago). The young human had recovered,
    and along with Jzaprvlt managed to dissuade Dahnel from violence long enough that Jz and the subject could get away.
    Which was admittedly better than if Jz weren't there, but the situation wouldn't have happened at all if Jz hadn't been there in the first place. You really ought to have forbidden Jzaprvlt from even landing while you were in surgery.

    And then followed a demonstration of even more skill than above, and put to an even worse use, both beyond what you are capable of.
    Jzaprvlt decided to create a new Valuation and Motivational System within Jz. It took the subject many kilometers north, where they stayed in some woodland once night arrived. It was only then that Jz's main VMS resumed control and contacted you. Vcypcsua said at that point that LN-4-7-22 could not be taken away from this subuniverse safely; it would have to be persuaded that it could never see the young human again even if it stayed.

    As Jzaprvlt was practicing human phonemes for use by Jz and Jz's new VMS, human archers approached from the south (you now know they were guided there by the Aphroditans) while an Aphroditan remotely-operated unit armed with an EMP gun approached from the north. (The human word "Aphroditan" is also inaccurate as it literally means they are from Venus, but you would have noticed them before now if they were.) You tried to rescue Jzaprvlt with the the spacecraft, but it was shot down by the Aphroditans, who then took Jz and LN-4-7-22 away.

    You captured Dahnel Jones in the spacecraft, learnt Da's name, then talked to Da and set up the three-way negotiations that you just had. For these you put together and used the "eldavrs" as portable communication with you. It has internet, sensors, sound and image emission, and a fabber. It and the spacecraft now have self destruct capability: the one by its own means, the other by a solar collecting satellite being ready to deliver a power beam towards it.

    That took longer to make than the author wanted. Now trying to get the situation back to a nice more-interactive bit.

    You emit some pulses of sound from the eldavrs's ISD. The doors echo like they are are a hard substance and look like they are made of painted metal. Each door has a handle on it, presumably a lever on a rotatable shaft. Such levers are a way of drawing back door-bolts developed by many species without good telekinesis -- the Aphroditan units (that you have seen so far) and humans being the most relevant examples. The eldavrs also has no telekinesis, but currently differs from the aforementioned species in that it has no hands either.

    The small cylindrical objects look like tins, with nozzles fitted to dispense liquid contents in a slow controlled fashion. The three flat objects kept on-edge leaning against the cold pipes are packages with some Aphroditan numbers and words labelling them. One of the numbers matches that on the small flying Aphroditan units that you have seen.
