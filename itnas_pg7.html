You perceive a collection of entities that do not correspond to sensory input, but are caused by the perturbation of your brain by the helmet. You recognise only four of them: the three pattern-slices you were working on a while back, and the equipment to which you are connected. The supercomputer must be deriving aspects of your mind from your brain signals, thus providing an mechanism for viewing and inserting concepts. The concepts you do not recognise must be not similar to anything you have previously encountered.

Though you cannot contact the parts of your mind that are not within your skull, alternatives are being simulated. These virtual modules are not trained to do any particular conversion of lower-level information, so are performing a random conversion. To train these virtual lower-level modules, you must interact with the entities you are currently experiencing.

The reason for the necessity of the secrecy of this project is now apparent. Were its existence to become more widely-known, too many citizens and resources would be diverted towards it, leading to slower progress.
